# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make this project a welcoming, safe, and equitable space for everyone who participates in good faith.

We value participation from people of all backgrounds, identities, experience levels, and perspectives. We expect every participant to treat others with respect and to act in ways that help the project remain healthy and professional.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respecting different viewpoints and experiences.
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback with care.
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or derogatory comments.
- Sexualized language, imagery, attention, or advances.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, events, and any public representation of the project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Possible actions include:

1. **Correction** — A clear notice describing the behavior and the expected change.
2. **Warning** — A formal warning for a single incident or repeated behavior.
3. **Temporary restriction** — A time-limited restriction from project interaction.
4. **Permanent restriction** — A permanent restriction from project spaces for severe or repeated violations.

Reports may be made through the repository's private reporting or moderation channels. Maintainers will review reports promptly, handle them fairly, and protect reporter privacy to the extent permitted by the platform and applicable law.
